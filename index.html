Catch a fish

The white shark is a predator that eats fish, sea turtles, birds, and small whales. It hunts seals and sea lions, which are fat and nutritious. It uses stealth and speed to catch its prey. It can scavenge dead animals and garbage. The white shark lives in the coastal waters of all the major oceans, from tropical to temperate zones. It can migrate and dive deep, but it usually stays near the surface. It returns to the same feeding areas every year, where it may meet other white sharks.
